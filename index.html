About Me

Hello, my name is Chelsea Peter. I was born in Riverside, California, and grew up in Hanford. Growing up, we moved around a lot, and I attended several different schools. After graduating high school, I got married and moved to New York due to my husband's service in the Army.

We lived there for two and a half years before moving back to Visalia in 2014, shortly after I had my first child. That's when I decided to go back to school and pursue a career in the medical field.

In 2017, I had my first daughter. After her birth, I reconnected with a deaf friend and realized I was struggling to understand her. That experience reignited my interest in ASL, so I enrolled in ASL 1 to learn the basics.

Although my friend eventually moved away and we lost contact, my passion for ASL stayed with me. In 2019, I had my youngest child and began using ASL more regularly. I even started teaching my kids basic signs to help us communicate more easily.

Eventually, I returned to COS to pursue a degree in ASL. In 2024, I adopted my youngest child, someone I had known since birth. After her father passed away, I welcomed her into our family as one of our own.

Now, I'm proud to say I'm graduating this May with my Associate of Arts degree in ASL. In my spare time, I enjoy reading, spending time with my family, taking road trips, and visiting the beach, which has become my peaceful escape from the everyday world.
